Mogens Jensen, born October 31st 1963 in Nykøbing Mors, son of salesman Harry Jensen and home care assistant Ebba Møller Jensen.


Member period
Member of Parliament for the Social Democratic Party in Western Jutland greater constituency from November 13th 2007.
Member of Parliament for the Social Democratic Party in Ringkøbing County constituency from February 8th 2005 to November 13th 2007.
Candidate for the Social Democratic Party in Herning South nomination district from 2007.
Candidate for the Social Democratic Party in Herning nomination district from 2003 to 2006.

Parliamentary career
Minister for Trade and Development Cooperation from February 3rd 2014 to June 28th 2015.
Member of the Public Accounts Committee from October 26th 2011 to April 17th 2013.

Culture and media spokesman. Vice-chairman of the  the Legal Affairs Committee. Member of the Cultural Affairs Committee, the Foreign Affairs Committee, the Foreign Policy Committee, the Greenland Committee, the Faroe Islands Committee and the Nordic Council. Vice-chairman of the Danish Delegation to the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe. Chairman of the parliamentary group of the Social Democratic Party 2011-2012.  Vice-chairman of the Social Democratic Party from 2012.


Education and jobs
Trade union management course, the Danish Trade Union Congress School, Helsingør, from 1997 to 1999.
Matriculated, Morsø Upper Secondary School, from 1979 to 1982.
Primary school ('Folkeskole'), Nykøbing Mors Primary and Lower Secondary School, from 1970 to 1979.

Consultant, the Danish Confederation of Trade Unions, from 1987 to 2005.
Consultant on, the National Federation of the Workers' Educational Association (AOF), from 1986 to 1987.
Cultural affairs consultant, the National Federation of the Workers' Educational Association, from 1985 to 1986.
Secretary for education, the National Federation of the Young Social Democrats, from 1982 to 1985.


Affiliations
Chairman of Danmarks Socialdemokratiske Ungdom (Denmark's Young Social Democrats) on the island of Mors and in Viborg Amt (county), 1979-1982. Member of the Board of the Danish Upper Secondary School Students Union (DGS), 1981-1982. Member of the Board of ARTE (ticket sales), 1982-1985. Member of the Social Democratic Party's cultural committee, 1982-1986. HK (trade union for salaried employees) shop steward, 1983-1985 Member of the national executive of Danmarks Socialdemokratiske Ungdom (Denmark's Young Social Democrats), 1986-1988. Member of the Board of "Kunst på Arbejdspladsen (an art-for-hire association) from 1998. Member of the Board of the Bjørn Afzelius International Cultural Foundation (BAIK), 2003-2010. Member of the Board of Representatives of The Danish Arts Foundation, 2005-2012. A "Mors Ambassador" nominated by Morsø Municipality from 2007 Member of the Board of Jobbanken (organisation which seeks to reintegrate mentally vulnerable people into the labour market), 2007-2014. Member of the Board of Laugesens Have (conference centre), 2008-2014. Member of the Board of Kulturfonden Danmark-Grønland (Denmark-Greenland cultural foundation), 2009-2012. Member of the Board of Nordisk Kulturfond (Nordic cultural foundation) from 2015. Member of the Advisory Board of The Worker's Museum from 2015. Member of the Board of Arbejdernes Oplysningsforbund (AOF, a national organisation which runs evening and weekend courses) from 2015, Deputy Chairman from 2015. Chairman of The NORDEN Association (NGO) from 2015.


Publications
Author of the debate drafts »En samordnet ungdomsuddannelse« (Co-ordinated Youth Education), 1985, and »Menneskene skal blomstre« (People must Flourish), 1987. Contributor to the Danish Trades Union Congress 100th Anniversary book »I takt med tiden« (In Step with the Times), 1998. Various film and theatre productions.


Awards
Received the Upper Secondary School Teachers' Anniversary Award 1995.
